One of the most annoying aspects of playing a spellcaster in a d20 campaign is keeping track of your spells. It's an unfortunate duality that the source of your greatest power as a spellcaster is the one thing that slows you down as a player. You may have to keep track of which spell is recorded in which spellbook, which spells you have memorized today, how many have been cast, and how to present that information correctly (and honestly!) to your fellow players and DM.

Put it in your Grimoire and get back to gaming. Grimoire is a multipurpose list that caters to all d20 magic users. Using the increased potential of HTML5 and modern web technologies, it puts the magic back in spellcasting.

HTML5 Based: The latest standards in the HTML language allow more flexibility across device platforms, and unlocking the potential to truly be a web-based application, rather than just a web site, letting you interact and control your data.
Local Storage: Going beyond basic cookies, modern browsers allow you to store your data directly in the browser. The entire Grimoire application can run offline (after an initial visit to the site) and synchronize back to the server once you have connectivity again.
Connectivity: Share the read-only link with your fellow PCs and they can see you update your available spells in real-time.
 All Spellbooks: Arcane or Divine, Spontaneous or Prepared, Spells or Invokations. The problem with basic character sheets is they have to be one-size-fits all, and that makes recording spells cumbersome. Grimoire's spell lists adapt to all sorts of spellcasters, giving you the information you need, and hiding the rest, making it simple as a cantrip to understand.
 No Signups: No need to create an account, just create a new Grimoire. Use it simply on your own machine without anything else to configure, or take note of the administrative editing link to come back later and keep working on it. Grimoires that lay fallow for a length of time will be dispelled automatically.
